## Overview

This research directory contains comprehensive documentation on:

## Core Principles Emphasized

### The Romance Contract

**Two Non-Negotiable Requirements:**
1. **Central Love Story** - The romance plot is the main plot
2. **HEA or HFN** - Happily Ever After or Happy For Now ending required

**Reader Expectations:**
- Emotional journey
- Character growth through love
- Satisfying ending
- Believable connection
- Earned payoff

### Character Depth

**Heroes Must:**
- Be complex and flawed
- Have growth arc
- Become worthy of heroine
- Learn and change
- Earn the HEA

**Heroines Must:**
- Be complex and flawed
- Have growth arc
- Have agency
- Claim their happiness
- Choose love over fear

**Relationship Must:**
- Be equal partnership
- Show mutual growth
- Have believable chemistry
- Develop naturally
- Earn the commitment

### Narrative Purpose

**Every Element Must:**
- Serve the central love story
- Develop characters
- Build tension
- Explore themes
- Satisfy reader

### Reader Engagement

**Intellectual Challenge:**
- Will they end up together?
- How will they overcome obstacles?
- When will they admit feelings?
- Satisfying when resolved

**Emotional Journey:**
- Care about characters
- Invest in outcome
- Feel the tension
- Experience the growth
- Satisfaction at HEA

## Common Problems Addressed

### "Instalove"
- Problem: Love happens too fast, unearned
- Solutions: Build foundation, show progression, develop connection
- Reference: Pacing, relationship development sections

### "No Chemistry"
- Problem: Told they're attracted, not shown
- Solutions: Show physical reactions, internal thoughts, charged moments
- Reference: Chemistry building, relationship development sections

### "Weak Black Moment"
- Problem: Doesn't feel real, easily resolved
- Solutions: Root in established conflicts, make devastating, require growth
- Reference: Conflict and obstacles section

### "Unearned HEA"
- Problem: Declaration from nowhere, problems hand-waved
- Solutions: Show growth, resolve real conflicts, demonstrate change
- Reference: Character development, conflict resolution sections

### "Cardboard Characters"
- Problem: No depth, no growth, boring
- Solutions: Complex flaws, growth arc, internal conflicts
- Reference: Character archetypes section

## Development Workflow

### Phase 1: Concept
- Use romance bible template
- Define hero, heroine, their journey
- Identify subgenre and heat level
- Choose tropes

### Phase 2: Structure
- Choose framework (Romancing the Beat, etc.)
- Map major beats
- Plan conflicts
- Design relationship arc

### Phase 3: Character Development
- Develop each protagonist
- Map growth arcs
- Identify flaws and fears
- Plan chemistry

### Phase 4: Detailed Planning
- Complete beat planning worksheet
- Map intimacy progression
- Plan conflicts and black moment
- Design key scenes

### Phase 5: Drafting
- Write following structure
- Plant attraction early
- Build tension deliberately
- Earn emotional moments

### Phase 6: Revision
- Use revision checklist systematically
- Verify HEA requirements
- Test with beta readers
- Refine based on feedback
