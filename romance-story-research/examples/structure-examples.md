# Romance Structure Examples

## Classic Contemporary Romance (Romancing the Beat)

### Act I: The Setup (Chapters 1-5)

**Chapter 1: Opening Image**
- Heroine at work (she's a Type-A planner)
- Shows her controlled life
- Establishes what's missing (spontaneity, love)
- Reader connects with her

**Chapter 2: Hero Introduction**
- Hero shown (he's free-spirited artist)
- His chaotic, passionate life
- Establishes his flaw (commitment issues)
- Reader connects with him

**Chapter 3: The Meet Cute**
- They meet at wedding (she's planner, he's florist)
- Instant clash (order vs. chaos)
- Ataction beneath irritation
- Dynamic established

**Chapter 4: The Catalyst**
- Forced to work together on another wedding
- Can't avoid each other
- Professional proximity
- Story set in motion

**Chapter 5: Reaction/Refusal**
- Both resist attraction
- She thinks he's unreliable
- He thinks she's uptight
- Initial judgments established

### Act II: The Development (Chapters 6-15)

**Chapters 6-8: Bonding**
- Forced proximity working together
- Start to see each other's true selves
- Vulnerable moments shared
- Attraction grows despite resistance

**Chapter 9: First Kiss**
- Moment of weakness/spontaneity
- Chemistry undeniable
- Changes everything
- Stakes raised

**Chapters 10-12: Complications**
- His ex appears (external conflict)
- Her job threatened (external conflict)
- Both pull away due to fears
- Internal conflicts activated

**Chapter 13: Midpoint**
- They acknowledge feelings (to themselves)
- Intimacy deepens
- Both scared but committed to trying
- Stakes raised

**Chapters 14-15: Building**
- Relationship grows
- Intimacy increases
- External obstacles multiply
- Internal fears surface

### Act II Climax (Chapters 16-18)

**Chapter 16: Moment of Grace**
- Everything going well
- Planning future together
- Love within reach
- Reader knows it won't last

**Chapter 17: Lull Before Storm**
- Brief period of happiness
- False security
- Both start to believe

**Chapter 18: Black Moment**
- His ex returns with revelation
- Heroine misunderstands situation
- Hero's commitment fear activated
- He doesn't fight for her immediately
- She leaves, heartbroken

### Act III: The Resolution (Chapters 19-22)

**Chapter 19: The Aftermath**
- Both miserable apart
- Forced to confront fears
- Realize love more important than safety
- Growth occurs

**Chapter 20: The Grand Gesture**
- Hero confronts his fear
- Goes after heroine
- Declares love publicly
- Proves he's changed

**Chapter 21: The Surrender**
- Heroine forgives (shows her growth)
- Both vulnerable and honest
- Walls down
- Love acknowledged

**Chapter 22: HEA**
- Commitment made
- Future planned
- Both changed for better
- Reader satisfied

## Historical Romance (Regency)

### Structure Adaptation

**Chapter 1: The Setup**
- Heroine introduced (young lady, needs husband)
- Hero introduced (jaded rake, needs inheritance)
- Society constraints established
- Tropes set up (marriage of convenience)

**Chapters 2-3: The Meeting**
- Meet at ball/ton event
- Dynamic established (attraction + antagonism)
- Her family's financial troubles revealed
- His need for wife revealed

**Chapters 4-6: Marriage of Convenience**
- Circumstances force marriage
- Reluctant wedding
- Wedding night (tension but consummation)
- Awkward beginning

**Chapters 7-12: Developing Relationship**
- Forced proximity in married life
- Society challenges (scandal, reputation)
- Start to know each other
- Attraction grows despite resistance

**Chapter 13: Midpoint**
- Husband's past returns
- Wife feels betrayed
- Separation threatened
- Both realize they care

**Chapters 14-16: Complications**
- External villain (relative, ex, etc.)
- Society scandal
- Must work together
- Trust tested

**Chapter 17: Black Moment**
- Misunderstanding/betrayal
- Wife leaves (or threatens to)
- Husband must choose: pride or love?
- All seems lost

**Chapters 18-20: Resolution**
- Grand gesture (public declaration, saving from scandal, etc.)
- Vulnerability and honesty
- Love declared
- Society accepts them (or they defy society)

**Chapter 21: HEA**
- Expecting child (often)
- Love confirmed
- Future hopeful
- Regency HEA achieved

## Paranormal Romance Structure

### Act I: Introduction (Chapters 1-4)

**Chapter 1: Heroine's World**
- Normal world established
- Something missing
- May not know supernatural exists

**Chapter 2: Hero's World**
- Supernatural world established
- His nature (vampire, shifter, etc.)
- His conflict/curse

**Chapter 3: The Meeting**
- Worlds collide
- Immediate attraction (often fated mates)
- Danger or mystery brings them together

**Chapter 4: The Catalyst**
- Supernatural threat emerges
- Heroine pulled into his world
- Can't go back

### Act II: The Development (Chapters 5-14)

**Chapters 5-8: Learning**
- She learns about supernatural world
- They're drawn together (mating bond, etc.)
- External plot (supernatural danger)
- Internal plot (falling in love)

**Chapters 9-11: Deepening**
- Bond/attraction grows stronger
- Physical intimacy (may trigger bonding)
- Emotional connection develops
- Conflicts (his nature, her humanity, etc.)

**Chapters 12-14: Crisis**
- Supernatural villain attacks
- Both in danger
- Must work together
- Relationship tested

### Act III: Resolution (Chapters 15-20)

**Chapter 15: Black Moment**
- Supernatural conflict peaks
- Hero pushes heroine away (to protect her)
- She refuses to leave
- All seems lost

**Chapters 16-18: Confrontation**
- They face villain together
- Hero accepts love (and her risking herself)
- Villain defeated
- Supernatural conflict resolved

**Chapters 19-20: HEA**
- Bond completed (mating, marriage, etc.)
- Her choice to become supernatural (or not)
- Future together
- Paranormal HEA

## Romantic Suspense Structure

### Dual Plot Lines

**Romance Plot:**
- Follows standard romance beats
- Internal conflicts about trust
- Vulnerability through danger
- Love grows despite fear

**Suspense Plot:**
- Mystery or danger
- Investigation or running
- Villain identified
- Confrontation and resolution

### Structure Example

**Chapters 1-3: Setup**
- Romance: Meet cute (may be crime-related)
- Suspense: Crime committed/discovered
- Both plots established
- Protagonists thrown together

**Chapters 4-8: Development**
- Romance: Attraction grows, conflicts emerge
- Suspense: Investigation, clues, danger
- Both plots intersect
- Forced proximity

**Chapters 9-12: Complications**
- Romance: Intimacy, feelings acknowledged
- Suspense: Villain active, hero/heroine targeted
- Danger increases romantic tension
- Protect/be protected dynamic

**Chapter 13: Midpoint**
- Romance: Major emotional moment
- Suspense: Major revelation or attack
- Both plots escalate

**Chapters 14-16: Rising Action**
- Romance: Deepening feelings, fear of loss
- Suspense: Getting closer to villain
- Both intensify

**Chapters 17-18: Black Moment**
- Romance: Separation or near-death
- Suspense: Villain captures/threatens
- Both plots peak together
- All seems lost

**Chapters 19-20: Resolution**
- Romance: Reunion, declaration, HEA
- Suspense: Villain defeated, mystery solved
- Both plots resolved
- Commitment and safety

## Scene Type Examples

### The Meet Cute Scene

**Enemies to Lovers Meet Cute:**
- Professional rivalry established
- Immediate clash
- Banter and wit
- Attraction beneath irritation
- Sets up dynamic

**Example:**
- Two lawyers opposing each other in court
- Professional antagonism
- Sharp dialogue
- Physical attraction noted
- Reader sees potential

### The Building Tension Scene

**Forced Proximity Scene:**
- Stuck in elevator together
- Car breaks down
- Storm forces shelter together
- Working late in office

**Example:**
- Stuck in elevator during power outage
- Forced conversation
- Physical proximity creates tension
- Walls come down slightly
- Moment of connection

### The First Kiss Scene

**The Build-Up:**
- Leading with conversation or emotional moment
- Physical awareness described
- Leaning in, hesitation
- First touch, then kiss

**Example:**
- After emotional vulnerability
- Looking at each other differently
- Physical distance closing
- Heart rates, breathing noted
- Kiss happens, then reaction

### The Black Moment Scene

**The Devastation:**
- Misunderstanding or betrayal revealed
- Character's worst fear realized
- Relationship appears over
- Emotional devastation shown

**Example:**
- Heroine discovers hero's secret
- Feels betrayed
- Confrontation
- Hero doesn't explain well (fear activated)
- She leaves, he stays frozen

### The Declaration Scene

**The Realization:**
- Character realizes love
- Can't lose other person
- Must take risk
- Grand gesture or simple honesty

**Example:**
- Hero tracks heroine down
- Vulnerable speech
- "I love you and I'm scared but I choose you"
- She responds
- Commitment made

## Pacing Examples

### Fast-Paced Romance (Contemporary, Novella length)

**Timeline:** Weeks to months
**Pacing:** Quick attraction, fast development
**Beats:** Rapid progression through romance beats
**Heat:** Often higher, physical early
**Black Moment:** Quick, intense
**Resolution:** Fast but earned

### Slow-Paced Romance (Historical, Epic)

**Timeline:** Months to years
**Pacing:** Gradual development, extended pining
**Beats:** Each beat given room to breathe
**Heat:** Slower build, may be moderate
**Black Moment:** Extended period apart
**Resolution:** Deliberate and complete

### Moderate Pacing (Most Category Romance)

**Timeline:** 1-3 months
**Pacing:** Balanced, steady development
**Beats:** Standard spacing
**Heat:** Moderate progression
**Black Moment:** Standard duration
**Resolution:** Satisfying and complete

## Subgenre Variations

### Romantic Comedy Pacing
- Lighter tone
- Faster pacing
- More humor and banter
- Shorter black moment
- Quick, satisfying resolution

### Angst-Romance Pacing
- Slower, more emotional
- Extended internal conflict
- Longer black moment
- More dramatic resolution
- Emotional payoff higher

### Sweet Romance Pacing
- Focus on emotional intimacy
- Slower physical progression
- More development time
- Emotional resolution emphasized

---

**Key Principle:** Structure serves the romance story. Adapt frameworks to fit subgenre, heat level, timeline, and character needs. The emotional journey remains paramount.
