# Romance Subgenres Guide

## Major Romance Subgenres

### Contemporary Romance

**Setting:** Modern day, present time

**Characteristics:**
- Modern settings and issues
- Contemporary themes and problems
- Technology and social media
- Career and work-life balance
- Current social issues

**Popular Sub-Subgenres:**
- Romantic comedy (humor central)
- Workplace romance
- Small town romance
- Sports romance
- Holiday romance
- Cowboy romance
- LGBTQ+ romance
- Multicultural romance

**Heat Levels:** All ranges from sweet to erotic

**Reader Expectations:**
- Relatable modern characters
- Contemporary issues and themes
- Realistic settings
- Modern dialogue and sensibilities
- HEA required

### Historical Romance

**Setting:** Prior to 1950 (most commonly before 1900)

**Popular Periods:**

**Regency Romance (1811-1820):**
- British aristocracy
- Society and manners
- Marriage market
- Scandal and reputation
- Jane Austen influence
- Very popular subgenre

**Victorian Romance (1837-1901):**
- More rigid social structures
- Industrial Revolution
- Class tensions
- Gothic elements possible
- Social reform themes

**Medieval Romance:**
- Knights and ladies
- Castles and battles
- Arranged marriages
- Clan/family loyalty
- Rougher, more dangerous world

**American Historical:**
- Western/pioneer
- Civil War era
- Roaring Twenties
- Depression era
- World War eras

**Characteristics:**
- Historical accuracy balanced with romance
- Period-appropriate constraints
- Society and class issues
- Modern character in historical setting
- Research required

**Reader Expectations:**
- Period authenticity
- Historical details
- Social constraints as conflict
- Romantic escape to different era
- HEA required

### Paranormal Romance

**Definition:** Romance with supernatural/paranormal elements

**Characteristics:**
- At least one main character is supernatural
- May include fantasy worlds
- Supernatural problems/human problems parallel
- World-building important
- Species/culture differences

**Common Paranormal Types:**
- Vampire romance
- Werewolf/shifter romance
- Ghost romance
- Witch/sorcerer romance
- Demon/angel romance
- Fae romance
- Dragon romance
- Zombie apocalypse romance

**Reader Expectations:**
- Supernatural elements integrated
- World-building coherent
- Romance still central
- HEA required (may include immortality issues)
- Fantasy elements serve romance

### Romantic Suspense

**Definition:** Romance with mystery/thriller elements

**Characteristics:**
- Two plot lines: romance + suspense
- Mystery to solve or danger to face
- Often higher stakes
- Protect/be protected dynamic
- Danger creates forced proximity

**Common Elements:**
- Stalker/killer plot
- Amateur sleuths
- Professional investigators
- Cold case solved
- Witness protection
- Military/black ops

**Reader Expectations:**
- Both plots satisfying
- Romance central (not subplot)
- Mystery solved
- Danger real
- HEA required

### Fantasy Romance

**Definition:** Romance in fantasy setting

**Characteristics:**
- Fantasy world-building
- Magic systems
- May have quests/adventures
- Species/race differences
- Fantasy plot alongside romance

**Common Settings:**
- High fantasy (medieval-like worlds)
- Urban fantasy (modern world with magic)
- Epic fantasy (saving the world)
- Fairy tale retellings

**Reader Expectations:**
- World-building coherent
- Magic consistent
- Romance central
- Fantasy plot resolved
- HEA required

### Science Fiction Romance

**Definition:** Romance in sci-fi setting

**Characteristics:**
- Future or space settings
- Technology and science
- May involve aliens/AI
- Space travel
- Dystopian or utopian worlds

**Common Elements:**
- Space opera romance
- Alien romance
- Cyberpunk romance
- Time travel romance
- Apocalyptic romance

**Reader Expectations:**
- Science plausible (in world)
- Technology consistent
- Romance central
- Both plots resolved
- HEA required

### Inspirational/Christian Romance

**Definition:** Romance with religious/spiritual elements

**Characteristics:**
- Faith central to characters
- Religious themes
- Clean/sweet content
- Moral messages
- Prayer and scripture may be included

**Reader Expectations:**
- Faith treated respectfully
- No explicit content
- Religious elements authentic
- Romance central
- HEA required (often marriage)

### LGBTQ+ Romance

**Definition:** Romance with LGBTQ+ protagonists

**Categories:**
- Lesbian romance (F/F)
- Gay romance (M/M)
- Bisexual/Pansexual romance
- Trans romance
- Non-binary romance
- Polyamorous romance

**Characteristics:**
- Authentic LGBTQ+ representation
- May include coming out stories
- Queer community elements
- Social issues and acceptance
- Diverse relationships

**Reader Expectations:**
- Authentic representation
- Respectful treatment
- Romance central
- Real queer experiences
- HEA required

### Erotic Romance

**Definition:** Romance with explicit sexual content

**Characteristics:**
- High heat level
- Explicit love scenes
- Sexual exploration
- Kink and fetish elements possible
- Fantasy fulfillment

**Distinguishing from Erotica:**
- Romance plot still central
- HEA required
- Character development
- Emotional connection
- Story beyond sex

**Reader Expectations:**
- Explicit content
- Sexual exploration
- Still a romance story
- HEA required
- Consensual content

## Subgenre Conventions

### Reader Expectations by Subgenre

**Contemporary:** Modern, relatable, current
**Historical:** Period-authentic, romantic escape
**Paranormal:** World-building, supernatural romance
**Romantic Suspense:** Danger + love, both plots resolved
**Fantasy:** Magical world + romance
**Inspirational:** Faith-based, clean, moral
**LGBTQ+:** Authentic queer representation
**Erotic:** Explicit content, fantasy fulfillment

### Universal Requirements

**All Romance Subgenres Must Have:**
- Central love story as main plot
- Emotionally satisfying ending (HEA/HFN)
- Character growth through love
- Romantic tension and chemistry
- Believable relationship development

### Subgenre-Specific Elements

**Heat Levels:**
- Inspirational: Sweet only
- Historical: Varies, often moderate
- Contemporary: All ranges
- Erotic: High heat
- Paranormal: All ranges

**Time Periods:**
- Contemporary: Present day
- Historical: Various periods
- Regency: 1811-1820 specifically
- Fantasy/Paranormal: Any invented time

**Settings:**
- Small town contemporary: Cozy, community
- Urban contemporary: City life, career
- Historical: Period-specific
- Paranormal: World-dependent
- Fantasy: Magical world

## Choosing Your Subgenre

### Considerations

**What Stories Do You Love?**
- Which subgenres do you read?
- What time periods interest you?
- What heat level feels right?
- What elements excite you?

**Market Considerations:**
- What's popular in the market?
- What can you uniquely contribute?
- Where's your passion?
- What's commercially viable?

**Write What You Know/Love:**
- Authenticity comes from passion
- Research required for some subgenres
- Reader can tell if you're faking it
- Subgenre has conventions you must honor
- Innovation within boundaries

## Subgenre Blending

**Popular Combinations:**

- **Paranormal Romantic Suspense:** Supernatural + danger
- **Historical Romantic Suspense:** Historical mystery
- **Fantasy Romance:** Epic fantasy + romance
- **Contemporary Romantic Comedy:** Humor + romance
- **Time Travel Romance:** Historical + contemporary elements

**Blending Guidelines:**
- Romance still central
- Both elements serve story
- Don't neglect either
- Reader expectations for both
- HEA required

## Research Requirements

### Subgenre Research Needs

**Contemporary:**
- Modern careers/industries
- Current technology
- Social media/internet
- Current issues and slang

**Historical:**
- Period history and politics
- Social structures and mores
- Clothing and daily life
- Language and dialogue
- Historical events

**Paranormal/Fantasy:**
- World-building rules
- Species/culture details
- Magic/power systems
- Mythology and folklore
- Internal consistency

**Romantic Suspense:**
- Police/investigative procedures
- Weapons and tactics
- Legal procedures
- Crime scene details
- Realistic danger

## Subgenre-Specific Tropes

**Historical:**
- Marriage of convenience
- Rake redemption
- Governess/lord
- Arranged marriage
- Scandal and reputation

**Contemporary:**
- Workplace romance
- Small town romance
- Second chance
- Fake dating
- Grumpy/sunshine

**Paranormal:**
- Fated mates
- Bonding/marking
- Supernatural species politics
- Hidden worlds
- Prophecy

**Romantic Suspense:**
- Witness protection
- Stalker danger
- Amateur investigation
- Bodyguard protection
- Cold case

---

**Remember:** Know your subgenre's conventions, reader expectations, and market trends. Honor the genre while bringing your unique voice and fresh perspective. The central love story remains paramount regardless of subgenre.
