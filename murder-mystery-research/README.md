## Overview

This research directory contains comprehensive documentation on:

- **Character archetypes and roles** - Detective types, suspects, victims, and supporting cast
- **Plot structures** - Classic frameworks, pacing, and narrative organization
- **Evidence and clues** - Types, management, fair play, and distribution
- **Red herrings and misdirection** - False leads, distractions, and reader engagement
- **Storytelling techniques** - POV, dialogue, atmosphere, suspense, and style
- **Story framework tools** - Templates, worksheets, and checklists for development
- **Structural examples** - Concrete breakdowns of mystery patterns

## Core Principles Emphasized

### Fair Play
- All clues must be available to the reader
- No last-minute evidence
- Killer introduced early
- Solution traceable from clues
- Reader has same information as detective

### Character Depth
- No cardboard suspects
- Each character serves multiple purposes
- Characters drive the plot
- Emotional investment in outcome
- Growth and change

### Narrative Purpose
- Every element must serve story
- Red herrings must have additional purpose
- Subplots connect to main plot
- Setting reveals character
- Theme emerges through plot

### Reader Engagement
- Challenge but don't frustrate
- Surprising but inevitable solution
- Emotional and intellectual satisfaction
- Fair treatment of reader
- Reward careful reading

## How to Use This Research

### Before Writing

1. **Read the relevant sections** for your subgenre
2. **Use the story bible template** to plan your mystery
3. **Complete the clue planning worksheet** with your solution
4. **Review fair play principles** before drafting

### During Writing

1. **Reference structure examples** when stuck on plotting
2. **Consult evidence types** for clue ideas
3. **Use red herring techniques** when story feels too obvious
4. **Check pacing guidelines** if momentum flags

### During Revision

1. **Use the revision checklist** systematically
2. **Verify fair play compliance** with clue worksheets
3. **Test red herrings** against reader expectations
4. **Evaluate pacing** against suspense principles
5. **Get beta reader feedback** using provided questions

## Best Practices

### Planning
- Know the solution before writing
- Map all major clues before starting
- Plan red herrings that serve multiple purposes
- Design subplots that connect to main plot
- Establish character arcs

### Writing
- Plant clues early and naturally
- Develop each suspect fully
- Vary pacing deliberately
- Build suspense methodically
- Control information release

### Revision
- Test with beta readers
- Verify all clues explained
- Check red herrings resolved
- Ensure timeline consistency
- Confirm fair play

## Development Workflow

### Phase 1: Concept Development
- Use story bible template
