## The Solution (Writer's Eyes Only)

### The Crime
- **Victim:** Name, age, occupation, personality
- **Crime:** Murder method, time, location
- **Discovery:** Who found body, how, when

### The Killer
- **Identity:** Name, relationship to victim
- **Motive:** Why they did it (deep, personal reason)
- **Method:** How they committed the crime
- **Opportunity:** When they could do it
- **Cover-up:** How they hid their involvement

### The Solution Path
- **Key Evidence:** What proves guilt
- **Critical Clue:** What detective needs to find
- **Aha Moment:** How detective figures it out
- **Confrontation:** How killer is caught

## Plot Outline

### Act I: Setup

**Opening Scene:**
- What happens
- Why it hooks reader
- What's established

**The Crime:**
- When/where discovered
- Initial investigation begins
- Detective enters story
- First suspects identified
- First clues found

**End of Act I:**
- Detective committed to case
- Central questions established
- Initial suspect pool formed

### Act II: Investigation

**First Half:**
- Investigation deepens
- Each suspect investigated
- Clues accumulate
- Red herrings pursued
- Subplots develop

**Midpoint:**
- Major complication
- Stakes raised
- New development
- Detective's theory challenged

**Second Half:**
- Investigation continues
- False solution explored
- More complications
- Time pressure introduced
- Personal stakes increase

**End of Act II:**
- Major setback
- Everything detective thought is wrong
- Killer might escape
- Detective in danger

### Act III: Resolution

**The Realization:**
- Final piece falls into place
- Truth emerges
- Killer identified
- Method understood

**The Confrontation:**
- Detective faces killer
- Danger peaks
- Killer tries to escape
- Final revelation
- Killer caught

**The Explanation:**
- Complete walkthrough
- All clues explained
- Motive revealed
- Method detailed
- Red herrings explained

**Resolution:**
- Justice served
- Subplots resolved
- Characters' fates
- Detective changed
- Setup for next (if series)
