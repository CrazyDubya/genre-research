# Publisher Scenarios

Practical examples of applying Publisher protocols in various situations.

## Scenario 1: New Project Kickoff

### Situation
A new romantic suspense novel project is starting. You need to establish requirements and communicate them to the Writer agent.

### Publisher Actions

#### Step 1: Complete Publisher Brief

```markdown
## Project Brief: Rival Hearts

### Project Overview
- Working Title: Rival Hearts
- Genre: Romantic Suspense
- Target Length: 85,000 words
- Target Audience: Adult women 25-45, romance readers who enjoy suspense elements
- Comparable Titles:
  - "The Perfect Marriage" by Jeneva Rose
  - "Verity" by Colleen Hoover
  - "In a Hollow" by Owen Laukkanen

### Core Premise
Two rival attorneys forced to work together when they're both targeted by a killer connected to a case they opposed each other on.

### Requirements
[Full requirements as per requirement-setting framework]

### Milestones
1. Foundation (Week 4): Outline + Ch. 1-3
2. Act One Complete (Week 8): Ch. 1-8
3. Midpoint (Week 12): Ch. 1-15
4. Act Two Complete (Week 16): Ch. 1-22
5. First Draft (Week 20): Complete manuscript
6. Final Draft (Week 24): Revised manuscript

### Quality Standards
- Prose: Strong commercial fiction level
- Technical: Publication-ready
- Story: Tight plotting, dual tension (romance/suspense)
- Market: Fit for major romance/thriller lists
```

#### Step 2: Communicate Requirements

Provide Writer with:
- Complete Publisher Brief
- Genre research materials (romantic suspense guide)
- Comparable title analysis
- Quality standards documentation

#### Step 3: Verify Understanding

Confirm Writer can:
- Restate key requirements
- Identify critical milestones
- Explain quality expectations
- Ask clarifying questions

### Key Decisions Made
- Timeline: 6 months total
- Structure: Dual POV romance suspense
- Quality bar: Commercial publication ready
- Primary focus: Equal weight romance/suspense

## Scenario 2: Milestone Review (Significant Issues)

### Situation
Milestone 2 (Act One Complete) is delivered. Review reveals significant pacing issues - the suspense element is overwhelming the romance.

### Publisher Actions

#### Step 1: Document Findings

```markdown
## Milestone 2 Review: Rival Hearts

### Overall Assessment
Word count on target (21,500 words). Technical quality good.
CONCERN: Romance significantly underdeveloped relative to requirements.

### Strengths
- Suspense plotting excellent
- Opening hook strong
- Technical quality high
- Voice consistent and compelling

### Critical Issues
1. **Romance Imbalance**
   - Requirement: Equal emphasis romance/suspense
   - Current: Approximately 70/30 suspense/romance
   - Evidence: Only 2 romantic scenes in 8 chapters; character chemistry underdeveloped
   - Required Action: Add 2-3 romantic tension scenes; develop emotional connection

2. **Meet-Cute Weak**
   - Requirement: Compelling first encounter establishing chemistry
   - Current: First meeting is functional but lacks romantic tension
   - Required Action: Revise Chapter 2 encounter to add chemistry

### Important Issues
1. **Antagonist hint missing** - Need foreshadowing of threat by Chapter 3
2. **Internal conflict unclear** - Female lead's resistance needs strengthening

### Recommendation
Return for revision before proceeding. Scope: Moderate revision (estimate 1 week).
```

#### Step 2: Communicate Feedback

```markdown
Hi Writer,

I've completed the Milestone 2 review. The suspense elements are working beautifully, and I'm genuinely hooked by the central threat. The prose quality continues to be strong.

However, we need to address a significant imbalance before proceeding. The romance element is currently underdeveloped relative to our genre requirements. Specifically:

**Must address before proceeding:**
1. Add 2-3 romantic tension scenes distributed across Act One
2. Revise the Chapter 2 meeting to establish stronger chemistry
3. Develop the emotional connection between leads alongside the suspense

**Should address:**
1. Add subtle foreshadowing of antagonist by Chapter 3
2. Strengthen female lead's internal conflict about the relationship

I'm confident these additions will bring the manuscript in line with our romantic suspense positioning. Please revise and resubmit Milestone 2 within one week.

Questions welcome - happy to discuss specific scene suggestions if helpful.
```

#### Step 3: Adjust Timeline

```markdown
## Timeline Adjustment

Original Milestone 3 due: Week 12
Revision time needed: 1 week
New Milestone 3 due: Week 13
Buffer consumed: 1 week
Remaining buffer: 3 weeks

Impact assessment: Minimal. Early intervention should prevent compounding.
```

### Key Decisions Made
- Return for revision rather than proceed
- Specific, actionable feedback
- Timeline adjustment documented
- Offered support rather than just criticism

## Scenario 3: Quality Gate Failure at First Draft

### Situation
First draft delivered on time. Review reveals the ending doesn't deliver satisfying resolution - romance and suspense climaxes are separate and the romantic resolution feels rushed.

### Publisher Actions

#### Step 1: Assess Severity

```markdown
## Quality Gate Assessment: Milestone 5

### Pass/Fail Status: FAIL - Revision Required

### Issue Severity
- Technical quality: PASS
- Craft quality: PASS
- Story quality (Chapters 1-20): PASS
- Story quality (Chapters 21-25): FAIL
- Market alignment: AT RISK

### Core Problem
Romance and suspense plots resolve separately rather than integrating. Romantic climax occurs in Chapter 23, suspense climax in Chapter 25. Romance resolution feels rushed - 500 words following suspense resolution.

### Impact
- Genre expectations not met (romantic suspense requires integrated climaxes)
- Emotional payoff diminished
- Reader satisfaction at risk
- Market positioning compromised

### Fixability
HIGH - Structure can be revised without major rewrites. Requires:
- Interweaving climactic sequences
- Expanding romantic resolution
- Approximately 3,000-5,000 words of revision/addition
```

#### Step 2: Develop Revision Plan

```markdown
## Revision Requirements: Milestone 5

### Required Changes

1. **Integrate Climaxes (Critical)**
   Current: Separate romance (Ch 23) and suspense (Ch 25) climaxes
   Required: Interwoven climax where romance impacts suspense resolution

   Approach: Romance commitment should be integral to surviving/defeating threat. The emotional breakthrough enables the practical victory.

2. **Expand Romantic Resolution (Critical)**
   Current: ~500 words of resolution
   Required: ~2,000 words minimum

   Include: Meaningful conversation, emotional processing, future commitment, character growth acknowledgment.

3. **Connect Arcs (Important)**
   Ensure character growth from romance arc is demonstrated in suspense climax. The relationship changes both characters in ways that matter for the final confrontation.

### Word Count Impact
Current: 85,200 words
After revision: ~88,000-90,000 words (within acceptable range)

### Timeline
Original Milestone 6: Week 24
Revision time needed: 2 weeks
New Milestone 6: Week 26
Buffer status: Consuming final buffer

### Risk Mitigation
Schedule daily check-ins during revision period to ensure trajectory is correct.
```

#### Step 3: Communicate Constructively

```markdown
Hi Writer,

First, congratulations on completing the first draft. The suspense plotting throughout is genuinely gripping, and the character development arc is compelling. You've done strong work.

However, I need to flag a significant issue with the ending before we can proceed to final revision.

**The Core Issue:**
The romance and suspense climaxes are currently sequential rather than integrated. In romantic suspense, these should interweave - the romantic breakthrough should be essential to resolving the external threat. Currently, they feel like two separate resolutions, which diminishes both.

**The Romantic Resolution:**
At ~500 words, the romantic ending feels rushed after the emotional journey you've built. Readers invest in this romance for 85,000 words - they need (and deserve) a proportionate payoff.

**What I Need:**
1. Revise the final 5 chapters to integrate the climaxes
2. Expand the romantic resolution to ~2,000 words
3. Show how the relationship growth enables the final victory

I know this is significant revision late in the process. I'm confident it's achievable because your foundation is strong - we're optimizing an already good story, not fixing fundamental problems.

Let's schedule daily check-ins during this revision. I'm also happy to discuss specific structural approaches if that would help.

Target: Revised ending in 2 weeks.
```

### Key Decisions Made
- Quality gate enforced despite timeline pressure
- Specific guidance on what "integration" means
- Balanced acknowledgment of strengths with clear requirements
- Increased oversight during critical revision

## Scenario 4: Scope Creep Request

### Situation
Midway through Act Two, Writer requests to add a significant subplot involving the antagonist's backstory, estimating 10,000 additional words.

### Publisher Actions

#### Step 1: Evaluate Request

```markdown
## Change Request Evaluation

### Request
Add antagonist backstory subplot: 10,000 words

### Rationale Provided
Writer feels antagonist is underdeveloped and adding backstory will:
- Increase reader investment in threat
- Provide motivation clarity
- Add emotional complexity

### Assessment

**Pros:**
- Could strengthen antagonist
- Adds depth to suspense elements
- Writer clearly invested

**Cons:**
- Significant scope increase (12%)
- Timeline impact
- Risk of slowing pacing
- Not in original requirements

**Market Consideration:**
Current antagonist development is standard for subgenre. Additional development is nice-to-have, not must-have.

**Alternatives:**
- Enhance antagonist through existing scenes
- Single flashback chapter vs. subplot
- Imply backstory through dialogue/clues
```

#### Step 2: Make Decision

```markdown
## Change Request Decision

### Decision: PARTIAL APPROVAL

### Rationale
Full subplot would impact timeline and pacing. However, antagonist enhancement has merit.

### Approved Scope
- ONE dedicated antagonist POV chapter (max 3,000 words)
- Enhanced antagonist presence in 3 existing scenes
- Total addition: ~4,000-5,000 words

### Not Approved
- Multi-chapter backstory subplot
- Antagonist POV throughout

### Implementation
- Insert antagonist chapter after Milestone 3 (post-midpoint)
- Scene enhancements incorporated into regular development
- No timeline impact - absorbed into existing buffer

### Success Criteria
- Antagonist motivation clearer
- Threat feels more personal
- Pacing maintained
- Doesn't overshadow protagonist arcs
```

#### Step 3: Communicate Decision

```markdown
Hi Writer,

I've considered your request to expand the antagonist's backstory. I appreciate your investment in making this villain compelling.

**My decision: Partial approval**

I share your instinct that the antagonist could benefit from more development. However, a full 10,000-word subplot would risk:
- Pacing issues in Act Two (already the hardest act to pace)
- Timeline impact we can't absorb
- Shifting focus from our protagonist romance

**What I'm approving:**
- One antagonist POV chapter (max 3,000 words) positioned after midpoint
- Enhanced antagonist moments in 3 existing scenes
- Total addition: ~4,000-5,000 words

This gives you room to show (not tell) the antagonist's humanity and motivation while maintaining our pacing and timeline.

If you feel strongly that more is needed, let's discuss - but I'd need compelling justification for the pacing and timeline risks.

Please incorporate this into your Act Two development.
```

### Key Decisions Made
- Didn't simply reject - found middle ground
- Clear reasoning for limitations
- Specific parameters for what's approved
- Door left open for further discussion

## Key Patterns Across Scenarios

### Be Specific
Vague feedback creates confusion. Every issue should include what, where, and how to fix.

### Balance Accountability with Support
Enforce standards while offering help. You're a partner in success, not just a gatekeeper.

### Document Everything
Decisions, changes, rationale - all captured for reference and learning.

### Early Intervention
Address issues when small. Milestone 2 revision prevented much larger problems at the end.

### Protect the Work
Quality gates serve the final product. Better to delay than ship inferior work.

### Maintain Relationships
Clear, respectful communication builds trust. Writer accepted revision requests because they understood the reasoning.
