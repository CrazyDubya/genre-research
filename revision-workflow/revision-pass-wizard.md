# Revision Pass Wizard

A structured, multi-pass workflow to move a draft from big-picture fixes to line-level polish.

## How to Use This Wizard

1. **Gather inputs** (see below).
2. **Complete each pass in order**; do not skip ahead.
3. **Record outcomes** and open questions after each pass.
4. **Only move forward when the pass is approved**.

## Required Inputs

- **Draft manuscript** (full or per-scene).
- **Story timeline** (chronological events with dates/relative timing).
- **Canon file** (facts that must remain true).
- **Character voice guides** (voice traits, diction, tics, worldview).
- **Target audience + genre tone**.

## Pass 1: Structural (Plot/Arc)

**Goal:** Validate story architecture, stakes, and turning points.

**Checks**
- Core promise delivered (genre expectations + theme).
- Act/arc progression is clear and escalating.
- Inciting incident, midpoint, crisis, climax are earned.
- Subplots support the main arc.

**Outputs**
- Structural issue list with severity (High/Med/Low).
- Proposed reorders, expansions, or deletions.
- Scene-level change list (move/cut/add).

**Exit Criteria**
- Major plot holes resolved.
- Arc beats are trackable.
- The ending pays off setup.

---

## Pass 2: Character Voice

**Goal:** Ensure each major character reads as distinct and consistent.

**Checks**
- Voice markers (lexicon, rhythm, worldview) are consistent.
- Dialogue reflects power dynamics and intent.
- Internal monologue aligns with arc progression.

**Outputs**
- Voice deviations by scene.
- Per-character voice adjustment notes.
- Dialogue rewrite targets.

**Exit Criteria**
- Each major voice is identifiable without tags.
- No out-of-character actions or speech.

---

## Pass 3: Pacing

**Goal:** Maintain momentum and reader engagement.

**Checks**
- Scene lengths match tension levels.
- Tension, release, and escalation are balanced.
- Exposition is distributed and purposeful.

**Outputs**
- Sections to tighten, expand, or reorder.
- Flags for slow scenes and rushed scenes.
- Suggested beat splits or merges.

**Exit Criteria**
- No extended sagging sequences.
- High-tension beats land cleanly.

---

## Pass 4: Continuity

**Goal:** Eliminate timeline, canon, and logistics inconsistencies.

**Checks**
- Timeline events align with stated chronology.
- Canon facts (names, rules, world logic) remain consistent.
- Character knowledge matches what they should know.

**Outputs**
- Continuity error list (scene + fix).
- Canon discrepancies.
- Timeline conflicts and corrections.

**Exit Criteria**
- No conflicting facts or impossible sequences.

---

## Pass 5: Prose Polish

**Goal:** Improve clarity, rhythm, and concision without changing meaning.

**Checks**
- Sentence flow and readability.
- Word economy and redundancy.
- Style consistency with POV and tone.

**Outputs**
- Line-edit suggestions (tagged by toggle).
- Style guide adherence notes.
- Final cleanup list.

**Exit Criteria**
- Prose feels clean, intentional, and on-brand.
